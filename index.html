My name is Enya Liu, and I recently graduated with a Master’s degree in Biostatistics from the University of Washington. I have a Bachelor's degree in Computational Biology from University of Texas at Austin. After my undergraduate study, I worked as a bioinformatics engineer at Helix Life, where I analyzed microarray and RNA-seq data from GEO and TCGA databases using R and Python. I also worked as a research assistant on cystic fibrosis data at Seattle Children's Hospital, where I collaborated with clinicians to translate research questions into analysis-ready data. I cleaned and harmonized over 5,000 patient records across CHEC-SC and CFFPR using R, standardizing time windows, data structures, and treatment coding. I also developed a reproducible quality control and reconciliation workflow that identified and resolved over 15,000 data inconsistencies, reducing manual effort. In addition, I created summary tables, validation visualizations, and reproducible reports using R Markdown and Power BI to support clinical interpretation and data review.

I am broadly interested in Biostatistics and Bioinformatics, and I enjoy integrating domain knowledge with statistical modeling and programmatic data analysis.
